OUR MISSION

It is currently not easy to take people to the moon. The last manned mission to the moon was in 1972, and since then, no one has returned. However, there are a few different ways that people could potentially be taken to the moon in the future. One option would be for a government space agency, such as NASA, to develop a new spacecraft and launch system capable of transporting people to the moon. This would likely be a costly and time-consuming process, as it would involve designing and testing new technology and training astronauts to use it. Another option would be for private companies to develop lunar travel capabilities. Some companies, such as SpaceX, are already working on developing rockets and spacecraft that could be used for lunar travel. This approach could potentially be faster and more cost-effective than relying on government space agencies, but it would also come with its own set of challenges and risks. Overall, while it is possible that people will be able to go to the moon again in the future, it is not currently an easy or straightforward process. It will require significant resources, time, and expertise to develop the necessary technology and infrastructure to make lunar travel a reality once again.
